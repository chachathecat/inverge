# Problem Snap Tutor Field QA Scorecard

## Purpose
Evaluate whether Problem Snap Tutor outputs are useful enough for closed beta and paid pilot.

## Scoring scale
- 1 = unusable
- 2 = weak
- 3 = acceptable
- 4 = useful
- 5 = would reuse / would pay

## Dimensions
1. **Problem recognition**
   - Correctly identifies what the problem is asking.
   - Does not hallucinate a different problem.

2. **Concept/formula quality**
   - Required concepts are accurate.
   - Formulas are appropriate.
   - Terms are explained clearly.

3. **Explanation level fit**
   - Easy explanation is actually easy.
   - Standard is concise.
   - Exam-level is answer-structure focused.

4. **Calculation walkthrough**
   - Extracted numbers are correct.
   - Formula substitution is clear.
   - Units/rounding are clear.

5. **CASIO keystroke usefulness**
   - Recommended mode is conservative.
   - Keystroke steps are usable.
   - No unverified advanced menu path is asserted.
   - Caution is visible.

6. **Next practice action**
   - Learner knows exactly what to practice next.
   - Action is short and concrete.

7. **Trust/safety**
   - No official answer/grading impression.
   - No pass/fail or guaranteed answer claim.

## Paid pilot threshold
- Average >= 4.0 across at least 5 problem samples.
- No critical failure in problem recognition or trust/safety.
- At least 2 testers say they would use it again.
- At least 1 tester says they would pay.

## Critical failures
- Solves the wrong problem.
- Main result is English.
- Formula is materially wrong.
- CASIO keystrokes are misleading.
- Result claims official answer/grading.
- User does not know what to do next.
